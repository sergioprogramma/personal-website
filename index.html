Case Study

Elevating Sleep Monitoring in the Apple Ecosystem

Background

The Apple Watch seamlessly incorporates a sophisticated sleep tracking system that integrates multiple sensors and cutting-edge algorithms. Utilizing the accelerometer, it precisely captures movement nuances during sleep, while the heart rate monitor continuously records fluctuations throughout the night. The gyroscope enhances this by discerning body positions and detecting restlessness. Powered by advanced machine learning algorithms, the Apple Watch transforms this wealth of data into insightful analyses of sleep stages and overall quality. Users benefit from a user-friendly interface presenting comprehensive sleep metrics, including total sleep time and detailed insights into different sleep phases. Features like Wind Down automate pre-sleep routines, while personalized sleep schedules and long-term trends offer users a holistic view of their sleep behaviors.

Problem

The existing sleep tracking feature on the Apple Watch falls short of the comprehensive capabilities offered by competitors such as Fitbit. Notably, it lacks the ability to detect sleep apnea and provide an extensive trend analysis of sleep patterns over time. Unlike some competitors, the feature also misses out on offering suggestions related to environmental factors like room lighting and ambient noises, which could potentially impact sleep quality. This omission leaves users without insights into conditional elements that might influence their sleep experience, limiting the overall effectiveness of the current sleep tracking functionality.

Product Idea

About

Introducing an advanced sleep tracking feature for the Apple Watch that goes beyond traditional capabilities. This comprehensive system, accessible directly from the watch, enables high-intensity tracking of sleep-related data. Activated before bedtime, the feature records frequent data points such as heart rate, snoring, oxygen saturation, and body position. Leveraging the paired iPhone, the system detects snoring, monitors noise and light levels, assesses air quality, measures humidity, considers daylight savings, accounts for changes in time zones, and takes temperature into consideration. The collected data is then processed by a sophisticated algorithm, offering detailed insights into sleep stages, sleep quality classification, sleeping positions, snoring with timestamps, and a potential alert for sleep apnea detection.

Core Features
  • Detailed sleep report available, daily, weekly and monthly
  • Sleep apnea detection
  • Light and noise detection
  • Life style and sleep correlation data

Is

A detailed sleep tracking feature that also offers sleep apnea detection. The feature provides a high-intensity tracking feature on the Apple Watch to track sleep-related data. You enable the feature, and you can access it from the watch. The system utilizes paired iPhone capabilities to measure light and noise as well, providing a comprehensive analysis of environmental factors.


It provides a report the morning after as well as patterns of the sleep cycles to identify causes that might affect a negative sleep routine and vice versa. The product is also equipped with a feature to detect a high probability of sleep apnea. Additionally, the enhanced sleep tracking system uses your health and phone data to provide a more accurate system to identify things that alter your sleep.

Is Not

This tool does not provide a medical-level report, nor does it diagnose sleep apnea definitively; it merely suggests the possibility. It is not suitable for individuals under 18, and it lacks real-time warning capabilities.

Does

When activated before sleep, the Apple Watch records heart rate, snoring, oxygen saturation, and body position extensively. Data collection involves both the Apple Watch and the paired iPhone, with a focus on detecting snoring. The system monitors various sleep-related factors, including noise, light levels, air quality, humidity, daylight savings, time zone changes, and temperature. Drawing on past activity, it identifies potential hindrances to falling asleep, such as loud noises, phone usage, and workout sessions.


The algorithm processes this rich dataset to furnish detailed information on time spent in each sleep stage, a quality-of-sleep score ranging from 0 to 100, different sleeping positions, snoring occurrences with time stamps, and alerts for possible sleep apnea detection.

Does Not

The application refrains from providing medical-level data or advice. The alert it issues is not a definitive diagnosis but rather a suggestion to seek professional medical advice. The product conscientiously avoids sharing any sensitive or identifiable health data with third parties. Notably, it is not designed for monitoring children’s sleep. Furthermore, it does not address conditions such as asthma or bronchitis. Importantly, it is not intended to function as a blood pressure monitor.

Determining Value

Value Calculation

To understand the value of the new feature, it is essential to assess (scale from 0 to 5):

  • Population: Impact on how many customers
  • Need: Importance for those who require it
  • Strategy: Alignment with our company and product strategy
  • Effort: Amount of work required
  • Confidence: Level of confidence in each of the above scores

The formula (1.0 * Population + 1.0 * Need + 1.0 * Strategy - 1.0 * Effort) * Confidence, determines the value.

Result

    The formula (1.0 * 2 + 1.0 * 5 + 1.0 * 4 - 1.0 * 2) * 80% = 7.2

    Each point is worth $50M for Apple Inc.

    Value = 50M * 7.2 = $360M

    The value associated with the product is an attempt to estimate the feature by considering the assigned values of each point, comparing them to the values assigned by other health tech companies in proportion to their market value.
